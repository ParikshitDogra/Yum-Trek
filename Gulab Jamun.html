Gulab Jamun Recipe

Gulab Jamun is a classic Indian dessert made from khoya (milk solids) or milk powder, deep-fried and soaked in a fragrant sugar syrup. Itâ€™s sweet, soft, and irresistible!

Ingredients:

For the Gulab Jamun:

  • 1 cup khoya (or 1 cup milk powder)
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1-2 tbsp milk (to knead)
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • Ghee or oil for deep frying

For the Sugar Syrup:

  • 2 cups sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods, slightly crushed
  • 1 tsp rose water or a few strands of saffron (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp lemon juice (prevents crystallization)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Sugar Syrup:

  1. In a large pan, combine sugar and water. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Add the crushed cardamom pods and bring the syrup to a boil. Let it simmer for about 8-10 minutes until slightly thickened.
  3. Add rose water or saffron (if using) and lemon juice. Stir and turn off the heat. Set the syrup aside but keep it warm.

2. Make the Gulab Jamun Dough:

  1. In a mixing bowl, crumble the khoya (or if using milk powder, mix with a little milk) until smooth and lump-free.
  2. Add the all-purpose flour and baking soda. Mix everything together gently.
  3. Gradually add 1-2 tablespoons of milk to form a soft dough. The dough should be soft but not sticky. Do not over-knead.
  4. Divide the dough into small equal-sized balls (about the size of a marble). Make sure there are no cracks in the balls.

3. Fry the Gulab Jamuns:

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a deep frying pan over low-medium heat. The oil should be moderately hot (not too hot or the gulab jamuns will brown quickly but remain uncooked inside).
  2. Gently slide the dough balls into the oil in batches, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan.
  3. Fry the balls on low heat, stirring gently and frequently to ensure even browning. They should turn golden brown and expand slightly as they cook. This process may take 6-8 minutes per batch.
  4. Once golden brown, remove the gulab jamuns from the oil and drain on paper towels.

4. Soak in Sugar Syrup:

  1. Let the fried gulab jamuns cool for a minute, then transfer them into the warm sugar syrup.
  2. Let them soak in the syrup for at least 1-2 hours so that they absorb the syrup and become soft and juicy.

5. Serve:

Serve the gulab jamuns warm or at room temperature, garnished with a sprinkle of crushed pistachios or almonds, if desired.

Enjoy your soft and delicious gulab jamuns!
