Fried Bhindi (Crispy Okra) Recipe

Fried Bhindi (Okra) is a crispy, flavorful snack or side dish that's easy to make and loved for its crunch. Here's a simple recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 250 grams bhindi (okra), washed and dried
  • 3 tbsp besan (gram flour)
  • 2 tbsp rice flour (for extra crispiness)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp amchur (dry mango powder) or chaat masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Bhindi:

  1. Wash the bhindi and pat them completely dry using a kitchen towel. (This is important to prevent sogginess.)
  2. Cut off the tops and tails, then slice the bhindi lengthwise into thin strips.

2. Marinate the Bhindi:

  1. In a large bowl, add the besan, rice flour, red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, cumin powder, amchur (or chaat masala), and salt.
  2. Toss the bhindi strips in the mixture, ensuring that all the strips are well coated with the dry spices and flours. No need to add water; the moisture from the bhindi will help the coating stick.

3. Fry the Bhindi:

  1. Heat oil in a deep frying pan on medium-high heat.
  2. Once the oil is hot, gently drop the coated bhindi strips into the oil in batches.
  3. Fry the bhindi until they turn golden brown and crispy. Stir occasionally to make sure they cook evenly.
  4. Remove the fried bhindi with a slotted spoon and place them on a paper towel to absorb excess oil.

4. Serve:

Serve the crispy fried bhindi hot as a side dish or snack, with a sprinkle of extra chaat masala and a squeeze of lemon for added tang.

Enjoy your crispy fried bhindi with a meal or on its own!
