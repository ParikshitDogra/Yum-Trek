Sattu Paratha Recipe

Sattu Paratha is a delicious and nutritious flatbread made with sattu (roasted chickpea flour) and spices, popular in North India, especially in Bihar and Uttar Pradesh. Itâ€™s a wholesome meal, perfect for breakfast or lunch.

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • Water (as needed, for kneading)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil (optional)

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup sattu (roasted chickpea flour)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1-2 tbsp fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp ajwain (carom seeds) (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp mustard oil (or any cooking oil)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (if needed, to adjust the filling consistency)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Dough:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour and salt.
  2. Add water gradually and knead to form a soft and pliable dough. If desired, add 1 tablespoon of oil to the dough.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 20-30 minutes.

2. Prepare the Filling:

  1. In another bowl, mix the sattu, chopped onions, green chilies, coriander leaves, ajwain (if using), cumin powder, red chili powder, mustard oil, and salt.
  2. Mix well until all ingredients are combined. If the filling looks too dry, you can add a little water to make it easier to handle, but be cautious not to make it too wet.

3. Stuff the Parathas:

  1. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls (about the size of a golf ball).
  2. Take one dough ball and flatten it slightly with your fingers. Use a rolling pin to roll it into a small disc (about 4-5 inches in diameter).
  3. Place 1-2 tablespoons of the sattu filling in the center of the disc.
  4. Bring the edges of the disc together to seal the filling inside. Pinch and twist to secure it, then flatten it gently.
  5. Roll it out again carefully into a paratha, ensuring the filling doesn't spill out.

4. Cook the Parathas:

  1. Heat a tava or skillet over medium heat. Once hot, place the rolled-out paratha on it.
  2. Cook for about 1-2 minutes until small bubbles start to appear. Flip the paratha.
  3. Apply a little ghee or oil on the cooked side and press gently with a spatula. Flip again and apply ghee or oil on the other side.
  4. Cook until both sides are golden brown and crisp, about 2-3 minutes total.
  5. Remove from the skillet and keep warm in a covered container. Repeat the process for the remaining dough balls.

5. Serve:

Serve the hot sattu parathas with yogurt, pickle, or chutney of your choice.

Enjoy your nutritious and flavorful sattu parathas!
