Siddu Recipe (Himachali Steamed Bread)

Siddu is a traditional steamed bread from Himachal Pradesh, often served with ghee or chutney. It is made with a yeast-leavened dough and has a soft, fluffy texture with a savory filling, typically made with ingredients like walnuts, poppy seeds, and herbs. Here's how you can make it:

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 cup warm water (for activating yeast)
  • 1/2 cup lukewarm water (for kneading)
  • A pinch of salt

For the Filling:

  • 1/2 cup walnuts, crushed (or poppy seeds or both)
  • 2 tbsp roasted sesame seeds
  • 1/4 cup grated paneer (optional)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tbsp fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Dough:

  1. In a small bowl, mix the active dry yeast and sugar in 1/4 cup warm water. Let it sit for 10 minutes until the mixture becomes frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and salt.
  3. Add the activated yeast mixture and mix well.
  4. Gradually add lukewarm water and knead the dough until it becomes soft and smooth.
  5. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 1-2 hours, allowing it to rise and double in size.

2. Prepare the Filling:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and green chilies. Saut√© until the onions turn soft.
  3. Add crushed walnuts, sesame seeds, grated paneer (if using), coriander powder, and salt. Mix everything well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add fresh coriander leaves and stir. Remove from heat and let the filling cool.

3. Shape and Fill the Siddu:

  1. Once the dough has risen, divide it into equal-sized balls (about the size of a small lemon).
  2. Roll each dough ball into a small disc, about 4 inches in diameter.
  3. Place 1-2 tablespoons of the prepared filling in the center of the disc.
  4. Gather the edges of the disc and seal it to form a stuffed ball. Flatten it slightly.
  5. Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling.

4. Steam the Siddu:

  1. Prepare a steamer by bringing water to a boil.
  2. Grease the steamer plates and place the filled dough balls on them, leaving enough space between each siddu.
  3. Steam the siddu for 10-15 minutes until they are cooked through and fluffy.

5. Serve:

Serve hot siddu with melted ghee or any spicy chutney, like mint chutney or tomato chutney.

Enjoy your Himachali siddu as a warm, hearty snack or side dish!
