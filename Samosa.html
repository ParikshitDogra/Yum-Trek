Samosa Recipe

Samosa is a crispy, flaky pastry filled with spiced potato filling, making it one of the most loved Indian snacks. Here’s a classic recipe to prepare it at home:

Ingredients:

For the Dough (Outer Covering):

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup ghee or oil
  • 1/2 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

For the Potato Filling:

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas (optional), boiled
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped (optional)
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp amchur powder (dry mango powder)
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds (saunf)
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil

For Frying:

  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Dough:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the all-purpose flour, carom seeds, and salt.
  2. Add ghee or oil to the flour and mix until the mixture resembles bread crumbs.
  3. Slowly add water, a little at a time, and knead into a firm dough. The dough should not be too soft or too hard. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

2. Make the Potato Filling:

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add grated ginger, chopped green chilies, and chopped onion (if using), and sauté for 2-3 minutes until the onion becomes soft.
  3. Add boiled green peas and sauté for another minute.
  4. Add mashed potatoes to the pan. Mix well.
  5. Add all the spices: coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, fennel seeds, and amchur powder. Mix everything thoroughly.
  6. Cook the mixture for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Add chopped coriander leaves and mix. Adjust the seasoning with salt as needed.
  8. Remove from heat and let the filling cool.

3. Shape the Samosas:

  1. Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls. Roll each ball into a circle (about 6 inches in diameter).
  2. Cut the circle in half to form two semi-circles.
  3. Take one semi-circle and fold it into a cone shape, sealing the edge with water.
  4. Fill the cone with the prepared potato filling, leaving some space at the top.
  5. Seal the open edges by pinching the top together, ensuring no gaps.
  6. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.

4. Fry the Samosas:

  1. Heat oil in a deep frying pan on medium heat.
  2. Once the oil is hot, gently slide the prepared samosas into the oil.
  3. Fry on medium-low heat, turning occasionally, until the samosas turn golden brown and crisp.
  4. Remove the samosas using a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.

5. Serve:

Serve hot with tamarind chutney, mint chutney, or tomato ketchup. Enjoy your delicious homemade samosas with a cup of tea!
