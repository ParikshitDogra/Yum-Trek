Palak Paneer Recipe

Palak Paneer is a classic North Indian dish made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked in a smooth and creamy spinach gravy. It’s healthy, delicious, and goes well with roti, naan, or rice.

Ingredients:

For the Spinach Puree:

  • 250 grams spinach (palak) leaves, washed and roughly chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic
  • 1-inch piece ginger
  • 1/4 tsp cumin seeds
  • Water for blanching

For the Gravy:

  • 200 grams paneer, cut into cubes
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp fresh cream (optional)
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) (optional)
  • Fresh lemon juice (optional)
  • Fresh cream for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1. Blanch and Puree the Spinach:

  1. Boil water in a large pan and blanch the spinach leaves for 2-3 minutes until they wilt.
  2. Remove the spinach and immediately transfer it to a bowl of ice-cold water to retain its green color.
  3. Drain the spinach and add it to a blender with green chilies, ginger, and garlic. Blend into a smooth paste and set aside.

2. Prepare the Gravy:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  5. Now, add turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, and coriander powder. Cook for 2 minutes until the spices are well roasted.

3. Add the Spinach Puree:

  1. Pour the prepared spinach puree into the pan and mix well with the masala.
  2. Add salt to taste and cook the spinach mixture for 4-5 minutes on medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. If the gravy is too thick, you can add a little water to adjust the consistency.

4. Add Paneer:

  1. Gently stir in the paneer cubes and let them simmer in the gravy for 3-4 minutes, allowing the paneer to absorb the flavors.
  2. Add garam masala and kasuri methi for added flavor. Mix well.
  3. If you like a creamier texture, add 1 tablespoon of fresh cream and stir.

5. Serve:

Garnish with a swirl of fresh cream and a squeeze of lemon juice (optional). Serve hot with roti, naan, or jeera rice.

Enjoy your delicious homemade palak paneer!
