Dhokla Recipe (Steamed Savory Cake)

Dhokla is a popular Gujarati snack made from fermented batter, usually of rice or chickpea flour. It's soft, spongy, and mildly tangy, perfect for breakfast or as a snack.

Ingredients:

For the Batter:

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1 tbsp semolina (rava or sooji) (optional, for texture)
  • 1 tsp ginger-green chili paste
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp lemon juice or 1/2 tsp citric acid
  • 1 tsp fruit salt (eno) or baking soda
  • 1 cup water (approx)
  • Salt to taste

For Tempering (Tadka):

  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds (optional)
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish
  • Grated coconut (optional for garnish)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Batter:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, sift gram flour and semolina (if using) to remove lumps.
  2. Add turmeric powder, ginger-green chili paste, sugar, lemon juice (or citric acid), and salt.
  3. Gradually add water to make a smooth, thick batter. Mix well to ensure there are no lumps.
  4. Just before steaming, add fruit salt (eno) or baking soda, and gently mix in. The batter will become frothy.

2. Steam the Dhokla:

  1. Grease a round steaming tray or thali with oil.
  2. Pour the prepared batter into the greased tray, spreading it evenly.
  3. Place the tray in a steamer or on a stand in a large pot with boiling water. Cover and steam for about 12-15 minutes on medium heat, or until a toothpick inserted into the dhokla comes out clean.
  4. Once steamed, remove the dhokla tray from the steamer and let it cool for a few minutes. Cut into squares or diamond shapes.

3. Prepare the Tempering:

  1. Heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add sesame seeds, curry leaves, and slit green chilies. Saut√© for a few seconds.
  3. Mix sugar in 1/4 cup of water and pour this mixture into the tempering. Let it simmer for a minute, then turn off the heat.
  4. Pour this tempering evenly over the steamed dhokla, letting the liquid soak in.

4. Garnish and Serve:

Garnish the dhokla with fresh coriander leaves and grated coconut (if using). Serve warm or at room temperature with green chutney and tamarind chutney.

Enjoy your soft and fluffy dhokla!
